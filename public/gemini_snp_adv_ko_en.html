Thoughtful Advice: Smart Strategies to Navigate the Waves of Uncertainty

First, Portfolio Review

In a 'Fear' environment like the present, it is crucial to reduce unnecessary risks and bolster your portfolio's defensive capabilities. Consider increasing your allocation to value stocks or dividend stocks that generate stable cash flow, rather than growth-oriented speculative stocks. Furthermore, avoid sector concentration and adhere to the principle of diversification to prepare for unforeseen shocks.

Second, Monitor Macroeconomic Indicators

Whether interest rates will be cut and signals of economic recession are key factors that will determine the overall market direction. Pay close attention to statements from the Federal Reserve (Fed), consumer price index (CPI), employment reports, and other major economic indicator releases, and cultivate the insight to anticipate the market's next moves based on this information. Data-driven decision-making is necessary, rather than premature judgments.

Third, Maintain a Long-Term Perspective

Rather than being swayed by short-term market volatility, it is important to set long-term investment goals and consistently pursue them. A 'Fear' phase can sometimes present opportunities to acquire quality assets at attractive valuations. Through consistent dollar-cost averaging, it is wise to view market downturns as opportunities.
